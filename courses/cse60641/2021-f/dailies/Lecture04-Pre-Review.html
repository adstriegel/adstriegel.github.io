Pre-Review Notes

As part of lecture, we will briefly walk through the following concepts:
  • Virtual Memory / Translation Lookaside Buffers (TLBs) - see OS3P Chapter 19
  • Context Switching - How does the OS switch between processes?
Consider the following questions that can be found in the papers directly or reading background materials:
  • Paper 3 - Exokernel
    • What is a TLB and what does it do?
    • What does it mean to switch between processes (e.g. context switch)?
    • What is file caching doing?
    • What is the concept of secure binding?
    • What is the concept of visible revocation?
    • What is the concept of an abort protocol?
    • Why is it important to perform authorization only at bind time?
    • What is a microkernel?
Consider the following questions in a group or by yourself (see above):
  • Paper 3 - Exokernel
    • Does appropriate hardware support exist or has it ever existed for an Exokernel?
    • Does an exokernel target multiprogramming or is it more geared towards single application systems?
    • Is it / should it be surprising that a well-tuned system outperforms a general purpose system?
    • When resources are plentiful, do the exokernel improvements matter?
    • How does the term microkernel, monolithic kernel, and exokernel relate to Nucleus and UNIX?
