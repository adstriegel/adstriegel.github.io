Pre-Review Notes

As part of lecture, we will briefly walk through the following concepts:
  • OS3EP - NFS
  • OS3EP - AFS
Consider the following questions that can be found in the papers directly or reading background materials:
  • Paper 16 - AFS
    • What is Venus? What is Vice?
    • What is a callback in the context of AFS?
    • When are changes propagated to the server?
    • When is the status of a file checked?
    • What is a cold cache? Warm cache?
    • How does AFS compare to NFS?
    • What is a volume in AFS?
Consider the following questions in a group or by yourself (see above):
  • Paper 16 - AFS
    • Can files become de-synchronized with AFS still?
    • AFS also provides improves ACL (Access Control Lists). What are the improvements to ACLs in AFS and why are they helpful?
    • If a system crashes or restarts, how does that impact AFS?
    • Does AFS preserve POSIX compliance from a file system perspective?
    • What would an invocation of an open file under AFS do? How would that contrast with NFS?
