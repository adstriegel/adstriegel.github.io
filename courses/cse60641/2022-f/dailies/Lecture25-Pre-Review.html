Pre-Review Notes

Consider the following questions that can be found in the papers directly or reading background materials:
  • Paper 27 - Xen
    • What is Xen's definition of paravirtualization?
    • How does the x86 handle TLB misses?
    • What is a hypercall?
    • What is a domain?
    • What is domain 0?
    • How many ring levels does the x86 have?
    • What is an I/O ring?
    • How are page tables handled in Xen?
  • Paper 28 - RT-Xen / Background
    • What does it mean to be a real-time system?
    • What is hard real-time? soft real-time?
    • What is EDF? RMS?
    • What is a period in the context of real-time systems?
    • What is a quantum? jiffy?
    • What is SpeedStep?
Consider the following questions in a group or by yourself (see above):
  • Paper 27 - Xen
    • Why is time potentially problematic with a VM?
    • Why is meeting time constraints hard with a VM?
    • Recall the purist / impurist discussion from Goldberg. Now having read several papers, who is right? Why?
    • What are the major differences and similarities between the VMWare approach and Xen?
  • Paper 28 - RT-Xen
    • What is the tradeoff with a lower quantum?
    • Why would disabling SpeedStep matter?
    • Why do the experiments share only a single core?
    • Is an event-based approach necessarily real-time? thread-based?
    • Is supporting hard real-time on Xen possible? Advisable?
