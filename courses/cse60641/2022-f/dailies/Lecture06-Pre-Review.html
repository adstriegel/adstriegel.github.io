Pre-Review Notes

As part of lecture, we will briefly walk through the following concepts:
  • Condition Variables - see OS3P Chapter 30
Consider the following questions that can be found in the papers directly or reading background materials:
  • Paper 6 - Events Bad Idea
    • What does it mean to block or yield in a thread? What is the difference?
    • What is cooperative multi-tasking? What is pre-emption? How are they different?
    • What does it mean to "call" by sending an event? How does a "return" work for events?
    • Is there a call-stack for events?
  • Paper 7 - SEDA
    • What is a stage in SEDA?
    • What is a queue in SEDA?
    • What is a well-conditioned service?
    • What is introspection?
    • What is the purpose of self-tuning in SEDA?
    • How are threads and stages related?
    • How is the number of threads in a stage controlled?
    • Where does batching come in and why is it potentially helpful?
Consider the following questions in a group or by yourself (see above):
  • 6 - Events are a Bad Idea
    • What does it mean to just fix events? Is it tantamount to switching to threads?
    • Why does the Duality paper helpful as discussed?
    • Are complicated patterns useful / helpful or are they largely academic exercises (Page 21)?
    • Will we ever get better synchronization help from the compiler or via run-time analysis? How big of a help will it be?
  • 7 - SEDA
    • Why can multiple threads be assigned to a stage?
    • Why do events scale differently than threads?
    • Is it scalability that limited the usage of threads (as SEDA argues) or the difficult in using them (as Ousterhout argues)?
    • Why is it important that the file being loaded in the scalability demo (threads vs. events) is already in the cache?
  • Both Papers
    • Has your opinion changed on who is right on events versus threads? Why?
