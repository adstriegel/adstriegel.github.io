  • Paper 21 - Multics
    • How does segmented memory differ from non-segmented memory?
    • How are files represented in Multics?
    • What is the relationship with pages and Multics?
    • Describe the hierarchy of access in Multics (DBR, DS, SDW).
  • Paper 24 - Survey Virtual Machines
    • What is a VM, VMM?
    • What are the major principles for virtual machines in the paper?
    • What are the major performance issues?
    • What are the major issues in practice?
    • What is the difference between privileged and non-privileged instructions? Could you give an example or at least a conceptual example of each type?
    • What is a SVC?
    • What does bare machine mean in the context of virtual machines?
    • Why do certain instructions need to be trapped and simulated?
    • How are the concerns for paging different than for I/O?
    • What is an f-map?
Consider the following questions in a group or by yourself (see above):
  • Papers 21 - Multics
    • Why is the arrangement of Multics for files / memory so critical?
    • How does it differ from a traditional UNIX approach?
    • Who controls DBR, DS, SDW definitions and access?
    • Compare and contrast a paged versus segmented approach to memory.
    • What are ring levels and why are there different ranges for R, W, and X potentially?
    • Can one ever execute something that one cannot read?
  • Papers 24 - Survey Virtual Machines
    • Had virtual machines finally arrived in 1974? Why or why not?
    • Should an OS that is in a virtual machine know that it is being virtualized? Who do you think is right, the purists or the imprints?
    • Is hardware changing as quickly as it was back in the 70s, 80s, and 90s?
    • Where is an area that VMs will always struggle? Where is an area that VMs will be better?
    • How could a course on OS or Grad OS leverage VMs?
    • What is the difference between a VMM and a hypervisor?
